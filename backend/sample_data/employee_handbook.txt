MERIDIAN TECHNOLOGIES INC.
EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK — HUMAN RESOURCES POLICIES & PROCEDURES
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Version: 4.2

This handbook applies to all full-time, part-time, and contract employees of Meridian Technologies Inc. ("the Company"). Policies are subject to revision; the most current version is always available on the internal HR portal at hr.meridian.internal.

SECTION 1: ONBOARDING CHECKLIST
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

All new hires must complete the following checklist within their first 10 business days. Your assigned HR Business Partner (HRBP) will track your progress and send reminders at the 5-day mark if items remain outstanding.

DAY 1 — Administrative Setup
  [ ] Sign and return your offer letter and employment agreement
  [ ] Complete Form I-9 identity and work authorization verification
  [ ] Submit direct deposit banking information via the Payroll Portal
  [ ] Receive and activate your company-issued laptop and access badge
  [ ] Attend New Employee Orientation (NEO) session — 9:00 AM, Room 204 or virtual link
  [ ] Meet with your direct manager for a 30-minute welcome check-in
  [ ] Review and acknowledge the Employee Code of Conduct

WEEK 1 — System Access & Compliance
  [ ] Activate your company email account (firstname.lastname@meridian.com)
  [ ] Enroll in Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) via the IT Self-Service Portal
  [ ] Complete mandatory Information Security Awareness training (approx. 45 min)
  [ ] Complete Anti-Harassment and Workplace Conduct training module
  [ ] Review the Data Classification and Handling Policy (HR-POL-014)
  [ ] Schedule introductory 1:1 meetings with key team members

DAYS 6–10 — Role Integration
  [ ] Complete department-specific onboarding training with your team lead
  [ ] Gain access to all required software tools (see IT Setup section below)
  [ ] Review your 30-60-90 day performance expectations with your manager
  [ ] Enroll in health, dental, and vision benefits (deadline: Day 10)
  [ ] Submit completed Emergency Contact form to HR

SECTION 2: REMOTE WORK POLICY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2.1 Eligibility
Remote and hybrid work arrangements are available to employees whose roles do not require continuous on-site presence. Eligibility is determined by the employee's manager in consultation with HR. Employees must have completed their first 60 days of employment before requesting a formal remote arrangement.

2.2 Hybrid Schedule
The Company operates on a hybrid-first model. Most roles are expected to be in the office a minimum of two (2) days per week, typically Tuesday and Thursday, unless otherwise agreed with the department head. Specific teams (e.g., Customer Support, Infrastructure Operations) may have different in-office requirements based on operational needs.

2.3 Remote Work Expectations
Employees working remotely are expected to:
  - Maintain their standard working hours as agreed with their manager
  - Be reachable via Slack and email within 15 minutes during core hours (10:00 AM – 3:00 PM local time)
  - Participate in all scheduled video calls with camera enabled unless a technical issue is documented
  - Ensure their home workspace is private, adequately lit, and free from disruptive background noise during client-facing calls
  - Never conduct company business from public Wi-Fi without an active VPN connection

2.4 Equipment and Expenses
The Company provides each remote-eligible employee with:
  - One company-issued laptop
  - A one-time home office setup stipend of $400 (claimed within 90 days of approval)
  - Monthly internet subsidy of $40, reimbursed via the expense portal

Personal equipment may not be used to process, store, or transmit company data unless formally approved through an IT exception request.

2.5 International Remote Work
Working from outside the employee's country of employment for more than 10 consecutive business days requires prior written approval from HR and Legal. Tax and employment law implications vary by jurisdiction and will be assessed on a case-by-case basis. Unapproved international remote work may result in disciplinary action.

SECTION 3: IT SETUP AND ACCEPTABLE USE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3.1 Standard Software Stack
All employees are provisioned with the following standard tools on their company laptop:
  - Operating System: macOS 14 (Sonoma) or Windows 11 Enterprise
  - Email & Calendar: Microsoft Outlook (Microsoft 365 Business Premium)
  - Collaboration: Microsoft Teams and Slack (Enterprise Grid)
  - Document Management: Microsoft SharePoint and OneDrive for Business
  - Password Manager: 1Password Business (mandatory use for all work credentials)
  - VPN Client: Cisco Secure Client — required when working off corporate network
  - Endpoint Protection: CrowdStrike Falcon (auto-installed, must not be disabled)

3.2 Software Requests
Requests for software not included in the standard stack must be submitted through the IT Service Desk portal. Requests are reviewed for security and license compliance within 5 business days. Employees must not install unapproved software on company devices.

3.3 Password and Authentication Policy
  - All passwords must be a minimum of 14 characters and include uppercase, lowercase, numeric, and special characters
  - Passwords must be changed every 180 days
  - Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all company systems
  - Sharing credentials with any other person, including colleagues, is strictly prohibited

3.4 Acceptable Use
Company-provided technology is intended for business purposes. Incidental personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with work duties or violate any provision of this policy. The following are strictly prohibited:
  - Accessing, storing, or distributing illegal content
  - Installing or running cryptocurrency mining software
  - Disabling or attempting to bypass endpoint security tools
  - Connecting company devices to unsecured or public networks without VPN
  - Using company email accounts to register for non-business services

3.5 Device Loss or Theft
Any lost or stolen company device must be reported to IT Security within two (2) hours of discovery by calling the IT Security Hotline at ext. 5500 or emailing itsecurity@meridian.com. Remote wipe will be initiated immediately.

SECTION 4: COMPLIANCE AND CODE OF CONDUCT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

4.1 Equal Employment Opportunity
Meridian Technologies is an equal opportunity employer. The Company does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other characteristic protected by applicable law.

4.2 Anti-Harassment Policy
The Company maintains a zero-tolerance policy for harassment, including sexual harassment, bullying, and retaliation. Any employee who believes they have experienced or witnessed harassment must report it to HR at hr@meridian.com or through the anonymous Ethics Hotline at ethics.meridian.internal. All reports are investigated promptly and confidentially.

4.3 Conflicts of Interest
Employees must disclose any actual or potential conflict of interest to their manager and HR in writing. This includes, but is not limited to, financial interests in competitor companies, employment of family members in roles that create a reporting relationship, and outside consulting engagements. Disclosure forms are available on the HR portal.

4.4 Confidentiality and Data Protection
All employees handle confidential business information and must comply with the Company's Data Classification Policy (HR-POL-014). Confidential information must not be shared with external parties without a signed Non-Disclosure Agreement approved by Legal. Employees are required to complete annual data privacy training as mandated by applicable regulations including GDPR and CCPA.

4.5 Mandatory Training Schedule
The following compliance trainings are required annually for all employees:
  - Information Security Awareness (due each January)
  - Anti-Harassment and Workplace Conduct (due each March)
  - Data Privacy and GDPR Fundamentals (due each June)
  - Anti-Bribery and Corruption (due each September)

Failure to complete mandatory training by the stated deadline may result in suspension of system access until the training is finished.

SECTION 5: EMPLOYEE BENEFITS SUMMARY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

5.1 Health and Wellness
  - Medical Insurance: The Company covers 85% of employee premiums and 70% for eligible dependents. Three plan tiers are available (Standard, Enhanced, Premier) through BlueCross BlueShield.
  - Dental and Vision: Covered at 80% for employees and 60% for dependents through MetLife.
  - Employee Assistance Program (EAP): Free, confidential counseling and support services — up to 8 sessions per year through LifeWorks.
  - Wellness Stipend: $600 per calendar year for gym memberships, fitness apps, or wellness-related expenses, reimbursed via the expense portal.

5.2 Retirement
  - 401(k) Plan: Employees are eligible to contribute immediately upon hire. The Company provides a matching contribution of 100% on the first 4% of eligible compensation deferred, with vesting after 2 years of service.

5.3 Paid Time Off
  - Vacation: 15 days per year for employees with 0–3 years of service; 20 days for 3–7 years; 25 days for 7+ years.
  - Sick Leave: 10 days per year, non-accruing, non-transferable.
  - Holidays: 11 company-observed federal holidays plus 2 floating personal holidays.
  - Parental Leave: 16 weeks paid leave for primary caregivers and 6 weeks for secondary caregivers, available after 6 months of employment.
  - Bereavement Leave: 5 days for immediate family; 2 days for extended family.

5.4 Learning and Development
  - Each employee receives an annual Learning & Development budget of $1,500 for conferences, courses, certifications, or books.
  - The Company maintains enterprise licenses for LinkedIn Learning and O'Reilly Online Library, available to all employees at no cost.
  - Tuition reimbursement of up to $5,250 per year is available for degree programs relevant to the employee's role, subject to manager and HR approval.

5.5 Additional Perks
  - Monthly commuter benefit: Pre-tax transit and parking up to $315/month
  - Company-wide volunteer day: 1 paid day per year for approved community service
  - Employee referral bonus: $2,000 (paid 90 days after successful hire)
  - Annual performance bonus: Target 10–20% of base salary, based on individual and company performance
