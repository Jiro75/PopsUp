ACME CORPORATION — EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK 2024
==========================================

Welcome to ACME Corporation. This handbook outlines the policies, procedures,
and expectations that govern your employment. Please read it carefully and
retain it for future reference.


SECTION 1: ONBOARDING CHECKLIST
---------------------------------

All new employees must complete the following steps within their first 30 days:

1.  Submit signed employment contract, NDA, and tax withholding forms to HR
    within 24 hours of your start date.

2.  Complete the mandatory Cybersecurity Awareness training course on the LMS
    portal within your first week. Failure to complete this course within
    30 days will result in system access suspension.

3.  Set up corporate email, Slack, and VPN access with the IT Help Desk.
    Submit an IT provisioning ticket at helpdesk.acme.com on Day 1.

4.  Attend the New-Hire Orientation session held every first Monday of the month
    in Conference Room A or via Zoom. Attendance is mandatory.

5.  Schedule a 1-on-1 introductory meeting with your direct manager within
    the first week. Discuss role expectations, 30/60/90-day goals, and team norms.

6.  Review and sign the Code of Conduct, Conflict of Interest Policy, and
    Acceptable Use Policy. Return signed copies to HR by end of Week 1.

7.  Complete the Benefits Enrollment form within 30 days of hire. Late
    enrollment forfeits coverage until the next open enrollment period.

8.  Obtain your building access badge from Security on Day 1. Badge requests
    require manager approval submitted via the facilities portal.


SECTION 2: REMOTE WORK POLICY
--------------------------------

ACME supports flexible work arrangements subject to the following conditions:

- Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week with written manager approval.
- A corporate-issued VPN must be active whenever accessing company systems remotely.
- Employees must maintain a secure, distraction-free home workspace.
- Remote workers must be reachable during core hours: 10:00 AM – 4:00 PM local time.
- Any remote work arrangement may be revoked by management with 48-hour notice.
- International remote work requires prior approval from HR and Legal due to
  tax and employment law implications.


SECTION 3: IT SETUP AND ACCOUNT PROVISIONING
----------------------------------------------

Day 1 IT Checklist:
- Laptop pickup from IT (pre-configured with endpoint security software)
- Corporate email account creation (firstname.lastname@acme.com)
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) enrollment — mandatory for all accounts
- VPN client installation and credentials
- Access to internal wikis, project management tools, and communication platforms
- Role-specific software licences requested via the software portal

All employees must:
- Never share passwords or access credentials with colleagues.
- Lock workstations when leaving your desk (Win+L / Cmd+Ctrl+Q).
- Report lost or stolen devices to IT Security within 1 hour.
- Use only approved cloud storage solutions (SharePoint, OneDrive).
  Personal cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox) is prohibited for work files.


SECTION 4: COMPLIANCE AND MANDATORY TRAINING
----------------------------------------------

The following training courses are mandatory for all employees:

1. Cybersecurity Awareness Training          — Due: Within 7 days of start date
2. Anti-Harassment and Workplace Conduct     — Due: Within 30 days of start date
3. Data Privacy and GDPR Fundamentals        — Due: Within 30 days of start date
4. Code of Business Conduct and Ethics       — Due: Within 30 days of start date
5. Health and Safety Induction               — Due: Within 14 days of start date

Role-specific training (assigned by manager):
- Secure Software Development Lifecycle (Engineering roles)
- Data Governance and Responsible AI (Data and Analytics roles)
- Financial Controls and SOX Compliance (Finance roles)
- Employment Law Refresher (HR and Legal roles)

All training is hosted on the LMS portal at learn.acme.com. Completion records
are audited quarterly. Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action.


SECTION 5: BENEFITS AND COMPENSATION
----------------------------------------

Health & Wellness:
- Medical, dental, and vision coverage effective from Day 1 of employment.
- Employee contributes 15% of premium; ACME covers 85%.
- Annual wellness allowance of $500 for gym memberships or fitness equipment.
- Employee Assistance Programme (EAP) providing free confidential counselling.

Paid Time Off (PTO):
- 0–2 years of service:    15 days PTO per year
- 2–5 years of service:    20 days PTO per year
- 5+ years of service:     25 days PTO per year
- PTO accrues monthly and can be carried over up to a maximum of 10 days.
- Public holidays: 11 federal holidays observed per year.

Parental Leave:
- Primary caregiver: 16 weeks paid parental leave
- Secondary caregiver: 6 weeks paid parental leave
- Adoption and foster care: same entitlements as birth parents

Retirement:
- 401(k) plan with company match of up to 4% of base salary.
- Vesting schedule: 25% per year over 4 years.
- Financial planning sessions available twice per year at no cost.


SECTION 6: PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT
-------------------------------------

ACME uses a continuous performance management model:

- Monthly 1-on-1 check-ins between employee and manager (mandatory).
- Mid-year review in June: progress against goals, development discussion.
- Annual review in December: performance rating, compensation review, promotion consideration.

Performance ratings:
  Exceptional  — Significantly exceeds all expectations
  Strong       — Consistently meets and often exceeds expectations
  Developing   — Meets most expectations; improvement plan may apply
  Needs Improvement — Does not meet expectations; formal PIP initiated

Employees who receive a "Needs Improvement" rating twice consecutively may
be subject to a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) or separation.


SECTION 7: CODE OF CONDUCT
-----------------------------

All employees are expected to:
- Treat colleagues, clients, and partners with dignity and respect.
- Maintain confidentiality of proprietary information at all times.
- Avoid conflicts of interest; disclose any potential conflicts to Legal immediately.
- Never engage in bribery, corruption, or facilitation of payments.
- Report suspected misconduct via the anonymous Ethics Hotline: 1-800-555-ETHICS.

Violations of the Code of Conduct may result in immediate termination and,
where applicable, referral to law enforcement authorities.


SECTION 8: ROLE-SPECIFIC ONBOARDING — ENGINEERING
----------------------------------------------------

In addition to the general onboarding checklist, Software Engineers and
Data Scientists must complete the following:

- Development environment setup guide (available on the Engineering Wiki)
- Code review participation in at least two PRs during the first two weeks
- Architecture overview session with Tech Lead (scheduled by manager)
- Access request to production systems requires two senior engineer approvals
- Completion of the Secure SDLC training before any production deployments
- Familiarity with the incident response runbook in the on-call documentation


SECTION 9: ROLE-SPECIFIC ONBOARDING — HUMAN RESOURCES
--------------------------------------------------------

HR team members must additionally complete:

- HRIS system access (Workday) — training session scheduled by HR Operations
- Review of all active Employee Relations cases within the first two weeks
- Meeting with each HR Business Partner to understand portfolio responsibilities
- Completion of Employment Law and Anti-Discrimination refresher training
- Understanding of payroll cutoff dates and the compensation review calendar
